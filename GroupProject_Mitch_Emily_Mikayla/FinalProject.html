Analyzing Wildfire Data in Alberta, 2000-2019

These maps show the 50 largest fires in 2 decades and the average maximum temperature, average minimum temperature, and total precipitation for that area.
The largest fires are indicated by the strongest colors and the color decreases as the size of the fire decreases.

Why is this important?

Forest fires can happen naturally, although the occurence and severtity of the fire can increase with climate change. It is useful to compare fire patterns over time to determine if changes are occuring.
Many forest fires are caused due to changing weather conditions. For example, dryer weather makes it more likely for fires to spread. The maps look at various climate data factors and compares them to the relative size of the forest fires.
